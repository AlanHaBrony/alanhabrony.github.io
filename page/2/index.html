Is it deterministic?

2024-05-01 21:31
Sometimes people like to say “He/She changed the world/game.”, while as the famous paradox, is He/She really changed the world or it is the World shaped him/her to change the world. In this question I prefer the later. Since when we talk about the history or events, we need a specific person to analyse. Well, does the specialness really matter? It’s related to generality and uniqueness. Since similarities are very common from DNA inside a person to the social environment outside, there are always many people of the same kind. But there will never be two person being the same, even if with cloning technique, the two individuals cannot maintain the same since there will not be two environments with no difference except for the “Parallel universe” which will conflict with the cloning setting, and once the two individuals know the existance of the other they have already been different, just like the movie Moon (2009).

2024-05-01 21:57
So I think the critical figures are “selected by the history”, in the sense he/she had the environment to develop the thoughts and skills, then as a member of the similiar group and just had the opportunity and a little random luck to stand 0ut at the phase transiation. So before the phase transiation there could be several possibilities, but the event only had fixed number of positions which were decided by the condition at that moment.

2024-05-01 22:09
I think it is easy to see the formation of a certain group is deterministic, because the group were grew within the similiar historical background and soical environment as the development of the social productivity. Then the specific individual part is more random as in the statistics behavior of Quantum mechanics.

2024-05-02 07:31
So the key point is whether two formation can be exactly the same, or whether a process/formation can rigorly recur. Since there are too many factors if we try to model all the details and again the one-direction property of the time arrow, I think this is the core difference between theory and reality, which might be not feasible.

2024-05-02 07:54
Back to determinism, because there are too many deterministic process in theory and reality and as the above consideration, we cannot use experiments to verify whether there can be different results for the same initia state. So again this is a question out of the testing abilities.

2024-05-02 08:04
By thinking, it is certain that the wills, wishes and thoughts guide a person’s behaviours, decisions and therefore the path of life. So in a purely ideal assumption, a will or wish can come true immediately and without any deviation. The difference is brought by the reality. Purely ideal assumption cannot be deterministic because it totally discount the reality.

2024-05-02 08:16
As a explanation of being deterministic, I think it is equavalent to a model with which if we input all the initial states and specify all the parameters then it can output the next state. I used to think this is a generalization in the 1800s without enough research in uncertainty. Regardless of the difficulty of full details, this leads the question to whether the uncertainty is deterministic, which seems contradictory. But the point is whether the uncertainty is really a property or in fact due to lack of deeper understanding. When I learned Probability and Statistics, I thought it is not directly useful when making decisions as the theory is based on the past observations and the enough sample size for CLT. So I think limit is in fact a compromise between theory and reality and because of which there can be various differences or deviations.

2024-05-02 08:32
As a cursory conclusion, I think it is deterministic if we have enough or all the information, initial states, variables, parameters and the ability to calculate based on all the inputs. And this brought another thought, if this condition was met then we could build a full detailed simulation of the reality. This made me wonder whether we could “build” a parallel universe under the assumption, and whether our universe is in fact built as such.
